SADDLE UP

IN JUST 10
SIMPLE STEPS

Grooming

First, you need to groom your horse to make sure they are clean and ready for the saddle. Be sure to use a combination of a curry brush, hard brush, soft brush, tail brush and hoof-pick as seen below.


Saddle Pad

While standing on the left-hand side of the horse, place pad(s) on the back of the horse. It should lie comfortably between the crest of their withers and hollow of their back.

English Saddle

Once the pad is in place, take the saddle with the stirrups rolled up, and align it with the pad. The arch of the saddle's pommel should be directly over the highest part of the horses withers (hover saddle).

Girth

The girth is first attached by taking the non-elastic side and connecting it to the saddle's billet straps on the right-hand side of the horse.

Cinching

Yehaaaw! You're halfway through the saddling process... it's almost time to ride! Now that we attached the girth on the right-hand side, safely move over to the left-hand side and finish securing the girth by pulling up on the leather billet straps. Make sure each side is on an equal hole.

Stirrups

Unroll and drop the left and right-hand sturrips before mounting.

Mounting & Adjusting

Mount your horse at a stable mounting block. It's always important check the girth before mounting and again after a few minutes of riding. Give your girth a second check for slack after a breif warmup.

Ready to Ride

Congrats! You are now ready to ride off into the sunset with your humble steed.
